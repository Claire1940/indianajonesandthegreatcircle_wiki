<Section icon={Search} title="Collecting the Winemaking Tiles">

Upon entering the hidden chamber, you will encounter a large mural on the back wall with five empty slots. To solve the **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle wine puzzle**, you must first scour the perimeter of the room to find the five missing stone tiles. Each tile depicts a specific stage in the production of wine, and their visual details are crucial for determining the correct chronological sequence.

Before picking up the tiles, examine the Mural Note found on a nearby crate. This note provides the essential hint: the process must be arranged from the beginning of the vine's life to the final storage of the wine.

<Callout type="tip" title="Exploration Tip">
Don't just grab the tiles and run to the mural. Take a moment to inspect the room for other collectibles, as this area often contains hidden notes and minor treasures that contribute to your total discovery completion.

<Section icon={Target} title="Solving the Vine Path Logic">

The most common mistake players make with the **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle wine puzzle** is assuming the tiles go in a standard left-to-right order (1, 2, 3, 4, 5). While the winemaking process itself has a clear chronological order, the mural's design adds a layer of complexity. If you look closely at the wall, you will see vines emerging from the ground and looping around the tile slots.

The "true" order is dictated by which vine leads to which slot. You must follow the physical path of the carved vines to understand which slot represents the first, second, third, fourth, and fifth step in the sequence.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the Process">
    Confirm the chronological order: Planting, Growing, Harvesting, Processing (treading), and Fermenting (amphoras).
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Trace the Vines">
    Look at the base of the mural. Follow the first vine as it winds up the wall to find where the "Plant" tile must be placed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Map the Slots">
    Note that the vines crisscross. The first vine actually leads to the first and third slots, while the second vine handles the second and fifth.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Place the Tiles">
    Insert the tiles into the slots based on the vine counting method rather than simple horizontal logic.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Visual Obfuscation">
The developers deliberately designed this puzzle to be counter-intuitive. If you place the tiles in logical order from left to right, the mural will not activate. Always follow the vine paths from the floor up to the slots.
</Callout>
