<Section icon={Shield} title="Technical Performance on Nintendo Switch 2">

Running **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle switch 2** is a demanding task for any handheld. On the Switch 2, the game utilizes DLSS (Deep Learning Super Sampling) to maintain a playable experience. While the visuals are impressive on a surface level—particularly the character likenesses—there are some technical trade-offs to be aware of.

| Mode | Target Resolution | Frame Rate | Tech Used |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Docked** | 1080p | 30 FPS | DLSS Upscaling |
| **Handheld** | 720p | 30 FPS | DLSS Upscaling |

Performance generally stays at 30 FPS, though minor stutters can occur during heavy action sequences or in larger sandbox areas like Giza. Texture pop-in and shadow resolution dips are the most notable visual flaws, but they rarely detract from the overall atmosphere. The audio design, however, is flawless, featuring a full score by John Williams and top-tier voice acting that brings the 1930s world to life.

<Callout type="warning" title="Performance Note">
Expect occasional frame rate drops in the later, more complex levels. The game is highly playable, but it is not a locked 60 FPS experience.
</Callout>
