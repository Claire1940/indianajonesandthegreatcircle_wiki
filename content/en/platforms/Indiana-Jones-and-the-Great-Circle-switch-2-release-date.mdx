<Section icon={Swords} title="Gameplay Mechanics and Combat Systems">

The gameplay in Indiana Jones and the Great Circle is a sophisticated blend of various genres. While it is primarily a first-person experience, the camera dynamically shifts to the third person during specific traversal moments, such as when Indy uses his whip to swing across chasms or scale ledges. This hybrid approach ensures that players feel immersed in Indy's perspective while still being able to appreciate the iconic silhouette of the character during high-action sequences.

Combat is designed with a "stealth-first" mentality. While Indy is capable of handling himself in a brawl, the game encourages players to use the environment and tools to avoid direct confrontations with large groups of enemies. You can use shovels, frying pans, and broomsticks as improvised melee weapons. The whip remains your most versatile tool, capable of disarming guards or pulling them toward you for a finishing strike.

<Section icon={Target} title="Puzzles and the Archeology Experience">

True to the spirit of the films, a significant portion of the game involves uncovering ancient mysteries and solving complex puzzles. These aren't just simple lever-pulling exercises; they require observation and the use of Indy's specialized gear. The camera is one of the most important tools in your arsenal. The puzzles vary in scale, from small environmental riddles to massive, multi-room "fantastical" puzzles that guard the game's most significant treasures. Solving these rewards players with Adventure Points, which are critical for character progression.

<Section icon={Cpu} title="Technical Performance on Switch 2">

The Switch 2 version of Indiana Jones and the Great Circle utilizes the increased power of the new hardware to maintain a cinematic look. However, there are technical trade-offs to be aware of. The game targets 30 frames per second, which provides a film-like motion but may experience occasional stutters in densely populated areas like Shanghai or the Giza dig sites. 

To achieve its resolution targets, the game employs DLSS (Deep Learning Super Sampling). While this keeps the image sharp at 1080p when docked, players may notice some "popping" of textures or shadows loading in late during fast-paced sequences. Despite these minor flaws, the character likenesses—especially for Indiana Jones and the antagonist Locus (played by Tony Todd)—remain remarkably high-quality.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Progression and Character Upgrades">

Progression in the Great Circle is tied to the discovery of Adventure Books. These books are hidden throughout the world and act as skill trees. Once you find a book, you can spend your hard-earned Adventure Points to unlock the specific skill it contains. This system encourages thorough exploration of every tomb and city you visit.

One of the most iconic upgrades is the "Hat Recovery" skill. In the films, Indy rarely loses his hat, and in the game, if you are knocked down, you have a brief window to crawl to your hat and pick it up. Doing so successfully grants you a second chance at life, effectively acting as an extra life that prevents you from reverting to the last checkpoint.
