<Section icon={BookOpen} title="The Lore of the Great Circle and the 17 Stones">

The narrative core of **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle Locus** revolves around the "Great Circle"—a series of ancient sites aligned across the globe. According to the lore provided by Emmerich Voss and Locus himself, these sites are not random. They are connected to the biblical myth of the Great Flood and the "Metatron," the voice of God.

The legend suggests that the Metatron instructed a mortal man (Noah) to build a vessel. To aid in this task and the subsequent repopulation of the Earth, Noah was given **17 Stones**, each imbued with the power to "fold the Earth," effectively allowing for instantaneous travel between the points of the Great Circle.

</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Encounters and Boss Mechanics">

Interacting with Locus often involves high-stakes combat or stealth sequences. Because of his massive size, traditional combat methods used against standard Nazi soldiers are largely ineffective. You cannot simply overpower Locus in a fistfight; you must use the environment and Indy's gadgets to survive.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Marshall College Break-in">
    During the initial encounter, focus on evasion. Locus is immune to standard punches. Use the environment to create distance and understand that this is a scripted loss designed to show his strength.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Vatican Escape">
    When Voss steals the stones, Locus acts as a secondary threat. Avoid direct confrontation. Use the whip to swing across gaps that Locus cannot easily cross.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Sukhothai Jungle Confrontation">
    This is where Indy and Locus finally come to an understanding. You must fight off waves of Nazi soldiers while Locus deals with the "Giants" of the site. Coordinate your attacks to protect his flank.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Final Stand">
    At the final site, Locus becomes an ally. While he provides a distraction by attacking the Nazi guards, Indy must focus on the puzzle mechanics of the Ark to prevent Voss from speaking the final "word."
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Strategy Tip">
When Locus is an ally, stay close to him. His massive health pool allows him to tank damage from heavy machine gunners, giving you the window needed to use your revolver or whip for precision takedowns.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Alliance: The Enemy of My Enemy">

The turning point for **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle Locus** occurs when Voss's true cruelty is revealed. Voss uses horse tranquilizers to subdue Locus and mocks his ancestral mission. This leads to a temporary alliance between Indiana Jones, Gina Lombardi, and Locus.

Locus realizes that Indy, despite being a "treasure hunter," respects the history and the danger of the artifacts. Unlike Voss, who sees the stones as a means to an end, Indy views them as pieces of a story that must be protected.
