<Section icon={Shield} title="Securing the Disguise and Royal Army Key">

You cannot simply walk into the **indiana jones and the great circle jungle bruiser pit** in your standard fedora and leather jacket. You need to blend in. The Royal Army uniform and the corresponding key are hidden within the compound's barracks.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Infiltrate the Back Wall">
    Once you reach the rear platform by swimming, use your whip to climb the wooden structures. There is usually one guard on this level; take him out quietly from behind to maintain stealth.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Locate the Barracks">
    Drop down into the interior courtyard. Avoid the open areas where multiple soldiers congregate. Look for a side door leading into the sleeping quarters or the storage room.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Find the Uniform and Key">
    Inside the barracks, you will find a locker containing the Royal Army uniform. Picking up the uniform will also grant you the **Royal Army Key**, which is essential for opening the locked gate to the pit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Don the Disguise">
    Equip the uniform immediately. While in this disguise, soldiers will generally ignore you unless you act suspiciously or enter restricted "No-Go" zones.
  </Step>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics in the Pit">

The **indiana jones and the great circle jungle bruiser pit** uses a specialized melee system that differs slightly from standard street brawling. To win, you must understand the balance between offense and defense.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="The Parry (LB)">
    The most important tool. Pressing the Left Bumper right before an enemy hit lands staggers them, opening a window for a counter-combo.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Stamina Management">
    Every punch and dodge consumes stamina. If you empty your bar, Indy becomes sluggish and cannot block effectively.
  </Card>
  <Card title="The 2-Punch Rule">
    Against tougher foes, never throw more than two punches at a time. This ensures you have enough stamina to parry the inevitable counter-attack.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

The rhythm of the fight is "Parry, Strike, Strike, Reset." Don't get greedy. The AI in the pit is programmed to punish players who spam the attack button. Wait for the visual tell—usually a slight pull back of the shoulder—before hitting the parry button.

| Action | Control (Xbox/PC) | Effect on Combat |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Light Attack** | X / Left Click | Fast strikes that chip away at health |
| **Parry** | LB / Q | Deflects damage and creates a stun window |
| **Dodge** | B / Space | Useful for unblockable heavy swings |
| **Finisher** | Y / F | Available when the opponent's health is critical |

<Callout type="success" title="Stamina Recovery">
If your stamina is low, back away and circle the opponent. Do not hold the block button, as stamina regenerates slower while in a defensive stance.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Opponent Breakdown and Strategies">

There are three fighters you must defeat to clear the **indiana jones and the great circle jungle bruiser pit**. Each fighter increases in difficulty and introduces new patterns.

| Fighter | Difficulty | Key Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Brawler** | Easy | Basic parry practice; he telegraphs heavily |
| **The Veteran** | Medium | Higher health; starts using multi-punch combos |
| **Skinny Pete** | Hard | Fast, agile, and counters if you over-extend |

**Defeating Skinny Pete**
Skinny Pete is the final challenge. Unlike the first two "bums," Pete has a high recovery rate. If you miss a parry, he will land a three-hit combo that can take half your health. The key here is patience. Wait for him to initiate, parry the first hit, land two light punches, and then immediately back off. Do not try to land a third punch, as he will almost always dodge it and counter-strike.
