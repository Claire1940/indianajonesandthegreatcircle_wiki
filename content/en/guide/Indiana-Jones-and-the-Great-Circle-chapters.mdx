<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat and Stealth Mechanics Across Chapters">

Throughout the Indiana Jones and the Great Circle chapters, you'll encounter various enemies and situations that demand a strategic approach. While combat is an option, the game heavily favors stealth. Indy levels up primarily through exploration and looting, not by defeating hordes of enemies. Understanding how to effectively use stealth and manage your resources will be crucial for survival.

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Management is Key">
Ammo for Indy's revolver is incredibly sparse, and using it will alert nearby enemies. Save your revolver for dire situations where stealth is no longer an option and you need to thin out a group quickly. Melee weapons also break easily, so always be on the lookout for new ones.
</Callout>

| Feature      | Stealth Approach                                   | Combat Approach                                       |

| **Benefits** | Conserves bandages, avoids damage, earns Adventure Points through exploration | Direct confrontation, can be quicker if executed perfectly |

| **Leveling** | Primary method for gaining Adventure Points        | Minimal impact on leveling, focuses on survival       |

The enemy AI in the early chapters is designed to be exploitable for stealth. Enemies will spot you, but you can use this to your advantage by luring them around corners for a silent takedown. Hiding bodies is essential to prevent other patrols from raising the alarm. The game also provides a useful lean function, allowing you to peek around corners while remaining hidden, a vital tool for planning your next move.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Exploration and Puzzle Solving: Core Chapter Elements">

Each chapter in *Indiana Jones and the Great Circle* is designed with intricate environments that require careful exploration and clever puzzle-solving. From ancient crypts to bustling city streets, you'll need to utilize Indy's unique skills to progress. This includes managing light sources, interacting with the environment, and deciphering clues to unlock new paths.

<Callout type="info" title="Torch Management in Dark Areas">
When in dark crypts, always light every brazer you find. They provide light, act as breadcrumbs, and allow you to relight lost torches. If you need to perform an action that makes Indy drop his torch (like whipping across a gap), throw it to your destination first to retrieve it easily.
</Callout>

Climbing is a significant aspect of exploration, but it consumes stamina. Always ensure Indy is rested before attempting a long climb. Eating fruit can provide a temporary stamina boost, while bread helps restore health. Keep an eye out for lockboxes; combinations are usually found nearby, or small locks can be shot open with your revolver.

<Section icon={Target} title="Progressing Through Chapters: Leveling and Objectives">

As you advance through the Indiana Jones and the Great Circle chapters, your progression isn't tied to a traditional skill tree. Instead, Indy's capabilities improve by finding unique adventure books. These books unlock specific skills, such as hitting harder, and require Adventure Points, money, or medicine bottles to activate. This system encourages thorough exploration and looting.

<Callout type="success" title="Maximizing Your Adventure Points">
Always keep an eye out for glowing notes or loot, as these often contain money, special items, or Adventure Points. The camera symbol in the top-left corner of your screen indicates a nearby photo op, which grants additional Adventure Points. If you're struggling to find collectibles, brochures can be bought from shops to mark them on your map, though they are expensive.
</Callout>
