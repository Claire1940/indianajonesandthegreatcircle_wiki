<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering Combat Mechanics">

Combat in the Great Circle is more than just mindless punching. It revolves around a delicate dance of parrying, stamina management, and using the environment to your advantage. On higher **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle difficulty** settings, a single mistake can lead to a quick game-over screen.

**Video Highlights:**
- **Parrying Basics**: Learn the timing for L1 blocks to stun enemies.
- **Whip Utility**: Using R1 to disarm enemies holding firearms or melee weapons.
- **Stamina Management**: Why attacking three times in a row is often a death sentence.
- **Environmental Kills**: Using chairs, guitars, and even apples to incapacitate foes.

The melee system relies on the "Hit and Run" strategy. Instead of staying in an enemy's face, you should land two hits and then dodge or back off to let your stamina meter refill. If your stamina hits zero, you become sluggish and cannot parry, leaving you vulnerable to the dreaded "triple punch" combo from Nazi soldiers.

| Combat Action | Button (PS5/PC) | Best Used When... |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Parry** | L1 / Right Click | An enemy's arm flashes or they begin a swing |
| **Whip Crack** | R1 / Q | An enemy is aiming a gun at you from a distance |
| **Charge Punch** | Hold Square / Shift+LMB | An enemy is blocking your standard attacks |
| **Dodge** | Circle / Space | A "Big Guy" enemy attempts an unblockable grab |

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Warning">
On Grandmaster difficulty, stamina regenerates significantly slower. Avoid sprinting immediately before entering a fight, as you will start the encounter with a disadvantage.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Solving the World's Toughest Puzzles">

The puzzles are the heart of the Indiana Jones experience. On the hardest puzzle difficulty, the game removes almost all UI assistance. You must rely on Indy’s journal, the camera, and your own deductive reasoning.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Document Analysis">
    Always read every page of a picked-up note. Some documents have a "Turn Page" prompt that reveals the second half of a code or a crucial map location.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Camera Tool">
    Use the camera to photograph suspicious wall carvings. Indy will often provide a written clue in his journal that translates ancient symbols into numbers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Language Barriers">
    Some puzzles, particularly in the Vatican, use Italian or Latin. Pay close attention to the spelling; a common pitfall is misidentifying handwritten letters like 'A' and 'O'.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Environmental Logic">
    Look for symbols on the floors or ceilings that match the icons on levers or pressure plates. If a door won't open, follow the "cables" or pipes to a power source.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Puzzle Type | Common Solution | Difficulty Spike (Hard) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Safe Codes** | Found in nearby letters or dates | Numbers are hidden in riddles, not plain text |
| **Lever Sequences** | Order of operations (1-2-3-4) | Order is determined by historical chronology |
| **Light Reflectors** | Angle mirrors to hit a gem | Mirrors are broken or missing parts |

<Callout type="info" title="Expert Insight">
If you find yourself stuck, check the Adventure Books. Some perks, like "Intuition," can provide subtle audio cues when you are looking at a relevant puzzle element, even on higher difficulties.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Stealth, Disguises, and Exploration">

In the Vatican, the Priest disguise allows you to walk past most guards, but swinging on a chandelier or running will blow your cover. In Giza, the Enemy Uniform is your best friend. However, keep in mind that "Captains" roam these areas. These elite NPCs have a white icon above their heads; if you stay in their vision cone for too long, they will recognize you regardless of your clothes.

<Callout type="success" title="Stealth Tip">
Toss items like bottles or apples to distract guards. This is particularly useful for moving a Captain away from a door you need to picklock.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="The Boxing Champions and Collectibles">

To truly master the game and earn the "Torto Force" trophy, you must defeat the boxing champions in each region. These are one-on-one brawls that test your mastery of the combat system.

**The Boxing Ring Strategy:**
1. **Patience is Key**: Do not swing first. Wait for the champion to throw a punch, parry it, and then land a 2-hit combo.
2. **Stamina Management**: If you exhaust your stamina, the champion will likely knock you out in one hit.
3. **No Healing**: Most rings forbid the use of food during the fight. Enter with full health.

| Region | Champion Type | Key Weakness |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vatican** | Heavy Brawler | Slow recovery after a missed heavy swing |
| **Giza** | Technical Boxer | Vulnerable to charge punches after a parry |
| **Sukatai** | Speed Striker | Low health; one well-timed combo can end it |

Beyond combat, the game is a "collectathon" with over 500 items. These include photos, relics, and adventure books. Collecting these is not just for trophies; adventure books provide the skill points needed to increase your health and stamina, which are vital for surviving the late-game encounters on Grandmaster difficulty.

<Callout type="warning" title="Glitch Alert">
Some players have reported that collectibles in Giza can bug out if you turn them in to NPCs too early. It is highly recommended to gather all regional collectibles first before completing the associated side quests.
</Callout>
