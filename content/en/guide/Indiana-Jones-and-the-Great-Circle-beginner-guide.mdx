<Section icon={Map} title="Mastering Indy's Core Tools">

The foundation of gameplay lies in three items: the Whip, the Camera, and the Journal. Mastering these early is essential for navigating the open-area hubs like the Vatican and Sukhothai. The whip isn't just a weapon; it is your primary means of reaching high ledges and swinging across deadly chasms.

| Tool | Primary Use | Pro Tip for Beginners |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Whip** | Traversal and Combat | Use it to trip enemies or pull them into a stealth grab. |
| **Camera** | Earning AP/Hints | Photograph everything; if the icon appears, it's worth points. |
| **Journal** | Maps and Codes | Review it often for safe combinations and puzzle clues. |
| **Lighter** | Lighting and Puzzles | Essential for dark tombs and activating light-based traps. |

The camera is arguably your most important tool for progression. Every photograph you take of a landmark, a suspicious document, or an ancient inscription adds a note to your Journal and grants Adventure Points. These points are the currency used to unlock powerful new abilities.

<Callout type="success" title="The Power of the Lens">
If you find yourself stuck on a puzzle, pull out your camera. Often, looking through the lens will highlight interactable objects or provide a hint through Indy's internal monologue, guiding you toward the solution.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Adventure Books and Progression">

Progression in Indiana Jones and the Great Circle is tied to **Adventure Books**. These are physical items found throughout the world or purchased from vendors. Once found, you must spend Adventure Points (AP) or Medicine Bottles to activate the permanent perks they provide.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Lucky Hat I">
    - **Cost**: 125 AP
    - **Effect**: Get a second chance in combat by picking up your fallen hat.
    - **Priority**: Must-have for early combat.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Pep I">
    - **Cost**: 275 AP
    - **Effect**: Significantly boosts stamina recovery speed.
    - **Priority**: Essential for climbing and boxing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Punch Out I">
    - **Cost**: 25 AP
    - **Effect**: Restores stamina when knocking out an enemy.
    - **Priority**: Best value for early brawling.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

Beyond AP, you will encounter **Medicine Bottles**. These are rare regional collectibles used specifically to unlock "Moxie" (Stamina) and "Shaping Up" (Health) upgrades. These are tiered, meaning you'll need more bottles for each subsequent level.

| Upgrade Tier | Medicine Bottle Cost | Permanent Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Moxie I** | 5 Bottles | Small Stamina bar extension |
| **Moxie II** | 10 Bottles | Medium Stamina bar extension |
| **Shaping Up I** | 5 Bottles | Extra Health reserve bar |
| **Shaping Up III** | 15 Bottles | Maximum Health capacity |

<Callout type="tip" title="Stamina is King">
Prioritize stamina recovery (Pep) and stamina extension (Moxie). Almost every action—climbing, sprinting, and boxing—drains your stamina. Having a larger pool allows you to explore more freely without constant pauses.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Stealth and Combat Strategies">

Combat is divided between stealthy takedowns and visceral first-person boxing. While Indy can use firearms, ammo is extremely scarce, and firing a gun will immediately alert every guard in the vicinity. Stealth is almost always the preferred approach in restricted zones.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Utilize Disguises">
    In regions like the Vatican or the Gizeh Nazi Compound, finding a disguise (like a priest's robes or a worker's uniform) allows you to walk through restricted areas without being attacked on sight.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Distraction Tactics">
    Throw bottles or use your whip to strike metal objects. This draws guards away from their posts, allowing you to sneak past or perform a silent takedown from behind.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Master the Parry">
    When forced into a fistfight, don't just mash the attack button. Watch for the enemy's highlight and parry their strikes. A successful parry opens them up for a high-damage counter-attack.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Whip Control">
    Use the **Throttle** skill once unlocked. This allows you to pull enemies toward you for a quick knockout, keeping the encounter quiet and efficient.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Enemy Type | Best Approach | Weakness |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Light Infantry** | Stealth Takedown | Low health, easily distracted. |
| **Heavy Soldiers** | Whip Trip + Boxing | Vulnerable to leg sweeps with the whip. |
| **Officers** | Disguise / Silent Takedown | Usually carry keys needed for progression. |
| **Boxing Champions** | Parry and Counter | Highly predictable attack patterns. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid the Alarms">
If a guard reaches an alarm bell, reinforcements will swarm your location. Always prioritize taking out guards near alarm stations first, or use your whip to sabotage the bells before starting a fight.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Exploration and Puzzle Solving">

The game features several large hub areas, most notably the Vatican, Gizeh, and Sukhothai. These areas are non-linear and filled with side content known as **Fieldwork** and **Mysteries**. Completing these is the best way to earn the AP needed for late-game upgrades.

**Key Puzzle Types to Expect:**
- **Light Refraction**: Use mirrors and Indy's lighter to guide beams of light into ancient sensors (common in Gizeh).
- **Weight Plates**: Place objects or canopic jars in a specific order to trigger hidden doors.
- **Environmental Riddles**: Read your Journal notes to find clues about saint statues or historical dates that serve as safe codes.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Achieving 100% Completion">

For players aiming for the Platinum trophy or 100% Steam achievements, the **Archivist** trophy is your final goal. This requires collecting every single Journal note in the game—a total of 497 entries. 

| Achievement Type | Requirement | Estimated Time |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Story Trophies** | Complete all 10 main Adventures | 15-20 Hours |
| **Fieldwork** | Complete all regional side-quests | 10-15 Hours |
| **Bookman** | Unlock all 55 Adventure Book abilities | 30+ Hours |
| **100% Cleanup** | Collect all Relics, Artifacts, and Notes | 45-55 Hours |

If you missed anything during your initial run, don't worry. After completing the final story chapter in Iraq, you can use the **Travels** tab in your Journal to return to any previous hub. All your progress, upgrades, and collectibles carry over, making post-game cleanup much easier.

<Callout type="tip" title="DLC Ready">
If you own the Premium Edition, you can access **The Order of Giants DLC**. This sends Indy to Rome to investigate the Cult of Mithras. We recommend finishing the main story first, as the DLC features some of the game's most complex puzzles and toughest combat encounters.
</Callout>
