The first safe you will encounter is located in the **Belvedere Court**. Upon reaching the open area, look for the first military tent directly in front of you. On the desk inside, you will find a ticket stub. By inspecting the front and back of the stub, you can piece together the four-digit code.

| Location | Clue Object | Safe Code | Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Belvedere Court Tent | Ticket Stub | **5238** | Combat Skill Book, Money |
| Post Office Cellar | Guard's Note | **1136** | Quest Progression, Loot |

<Callout type="warning" title="Enemy Presence">
The Belvedere Court is heavily patrolled. Ensure you clear the immediate guards or use stealth before attempting to inspect the ticket stub on the desk, as being spotted will interrupt the inspection animation.
</Callout>

Another critical location is the cellar beneath the post office, near the **Tower of Nicholas**. This is part of the **Savage Discovery** fieldwork. You must first obtain the Cellar Key from a nearby enemy. Once inside, take out the guard and read the note he carries. The note mentions "November last year." Since the game is set in 1937, "last year" refers to 1936, and November is the 11th month, giving you the code **1136**.

To unlock the Garden safe, you must find the "Secret Delivery Letter" which states a package arrived on October 15th. By checking the in-game calendar, you see that October 15th falls on a **Friday**. Cross-referencing "Friday" with the dice symbols on the calendar reveals the code **6380**. Inside, you will find a book detailing all medicine locations in the Vatican.

  <Step num="3" title="Turn Off the Light">

  </Step>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Sistine Chapel and Mystery Safes">

The Sistine Chapel holds two distinct safes, both tied to "Mysteries" that offer some of the best narrative rewards and skill boosts in the game.

The **Secret of Secrets** mystery is triggered in the back office of the Sistine Chapel. You will find a note instructing you to inspect the statues of **Peter (with the key)** and **Paul (with the sword)**. Turning these statues over reveals planetary symbols. You must then find the planetary chart in the room and order the planets by their distance from the sun.

| Planet Symbol | Order from Sun | Resulting Digit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Statue 1 Symbol | 4th Position | **4** |
| Statue 2 Symbol | 4th Position | **4** |
| Statue 3 Symbol | 7th Position | **7** |
| Statue 4 Symbol | 1st Position | **1** |

The final code for this complex puzzle is **4471**. This unlocks a mystery book and an additional skill book. Later, after completing the nun's questline, you can return for **Gina's Request** (Signs of Trouble). This safe is located in a singular building at the excavation site. The code, **1891**, is written clearly at the bottom of Gina's note.

<Section icon={Swords} title="The Father and Son Cipher">

Perhaps the most intricate safe is tied to the **Father and Son** mystery. This safe is located near the **Borgia Tower** in a side room that requires Juliana's Key to enter. Inside, you will find a cat, a dog, and a cryptic poem.

To solve this, you must loot the poem and look at the circled letters: **I, R, I, A, T, A, S, O**. You then need to compare these letters to a square diagram found in the room. Each letter or combination of letters corresponds to a specific number on the grid.

| Letter Group | Grid Coordinate | Resulting Digit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| I / R | Row 5 | **5** |
| I / A | Row 8 | **8** |
| T / A | Row 7 | **7** |
| S / O | Row 3 | **3** |

Following the sequence of the poem's circled letters leads to the code **5873**. Unlocking this safe concludes the Father and Son mystery, providing a significant boost to your adventure points.

<Callout type="success" title="Mystery Solved">
Once you input **5873**, the safe will open, rewarding you with unique lore items and finishing one of the most difficult puzzles in the Vatican.
</Callout>
