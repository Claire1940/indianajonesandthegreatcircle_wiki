To ensure a smooth and visually impressive experience with Indiana Jones and the Great Circle on PC, it's crucial to meet or exceed the specified system requirements. A key aspect across all tiers is the **mandatory requirement for hardware ray tracing**, which significantly impacts GPU selection.

<Section icon={Map} title="Maximizing Performance: Tips for All Platforms">

Achieving the best possible experience in Indiana Jones and the Great Circle involves optimizing your setup, regardless of whether you're on PC or console. Here are some general tips to get the most out of the game.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Update Drivers and System Software">
    Always ensure your graphics drivers (NVIDIA, AMD, Intel) are updated to the latest version. For consoles, keep your system software current to benefit from performance optimizations and bug fixes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust In-Game Settings (PC)">
    For PC players, experiment with graphics settings. If you're struggling to hit your target frame rate, try lowering settings like shadow quality, volumetric lighting, and anti-aliasing. Ray tracing can be demanding, so adjust its intensity if performance is an issue.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Ensure Adequate Storage (All Platforms)">
    Given the large installation size (126 GB base, plus DLC), make sure you have enough free space on your SSD. Fragmented storage can sometimes lead to minor performance hiccups, especially during loading screens.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Monitor Temperatures (PC)">
    Keep an eye on your CPU and GPU temperatures. Overheating can lead to thermal throttling, reducing performance. Ensure good airflow in your PC case and clean any dust buildup.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Close Background Applications (PC)">
    Before launching the game, close any unnecessary background applications, especially those that consume significant CPU, GPU, or RAM resources. This frees up system resources for the game.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
On consoles, while you have fewer direct settings to tweak, ensuring your console is well-ventilated and not obstructed can prevent thermal throttling, maintaining consistent performance.
</Callout>
