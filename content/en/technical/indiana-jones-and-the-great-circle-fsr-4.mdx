<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Understanding FSR 4 in The Great Circle">

FidelityFX Super Resolution (FSR) is a cutting-edge upscaling technology developed by AMD, designed to boost frame rates in games by rendering frames at a lower resolution and then intelligently upscaling them to your monitor's native resolution. FSR 4, the latest iteration, brings further refinements to this process, aiming for even greater performance gains with minimal visual compromise. For **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle FSR 4** means players can expect a smoother, more responsive experience, especially when pushing higher resolutions or maintaining stable frame rates on less powerful hardware.

<Callout type="tip" title="Why FSR Matters for Indiana Jones">
The game's visually rich environments and detailed character models can be demanding. FSR 4 acts as a performance multiplier, allowing a wider range of PC users to enjoy high frame rates without sacrificing too much visual fidelity. This is particularly beneficial for players targeting 1440p or 4K resolutions.
</Callout>

### Key Improvements with FSR 4

FSR 4 builds upon its predecessors with several advancements that directly benefit demanding titles like Indiana Jones and the Great Circle. The primary goals are to improve image quality, reduce ghosting and shimmering artifacts, and offer broader compatibility.

| Feature               | FSR 3.0 Comparison                                       | FSR 4 Enhancement                                                              |
| :-------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Upscaling Algorithm** | Good quality, some minor artifacts                       | **Advanced temporal upscaling**, significantly reduced artifacts, better detail |
| **Frame Generation**  | Included, but with higher latency potential              | **Optimized frame generation**, lower latency, smoother motion                 |
| **Image Reconstruction**| Effective, but could show slight blurriness              | **Sharper image reconstruction**, preserves fine details, less blur            |
| **Hardware Support**  | Broad, but best on AMD GPUs                              | **Even broader compatibility**, improved performance on all modern GPUs        |
| **Latency**           | Noticeable with Frame Generation in some scenarios       | **Reduced input latency** through refined algorithms and integration           |

To get the most out of **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle FSR 4**, understanding and adjusting the in-game settings is crucial. <Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Access Graphics Settings">
    From the main menu, navigate to "Options" then "Graphics Settings." Here, you'll find all the visual customization options for the game.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Enable FSR 4">
    Locate the "Upscaling" or "Resolution Scaling" option and select "AMD FidelityFX Super Resolution 4." If FSR 4 is not available, ensure your game and graphics drivers are updated to the latest versions.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Choose an FSR Preset">
    FSR 4 typically offers several presets: **Quality**, **Balanced**, **Performance**, and **Ultra Performance**.
    - **Quality**: Offers the best image quality with a moderate FPS boost. Ideal for high-end GPUs.
    - **Balanced**: A good mix of visual fidelity and performance. Suitable for mid-range systems.
    - **Performance**: Prioritizes frame rate over image quality. Best for maximizing FPS on lower-end hardware.
    - **Ultra Performance**: Provides the highest FPS boost but with a noticeable drop in visual clarity. Use only if absolutely necessary.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Adjust Sharpening">
    FSR 4 often includes a sharpening slider. A slight increase can help restore lost detail, but too much can introduce an artificial look. Start with a value around **0.5** and adjust to your preference.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Monitor Performance">
    Use in-game FPS counters or external tools like MSI Afterburner to monitor your frame rate and adjust settings accordingly. Aim for a stable FPS that feels smooth to you.
  </Step>
</Steps>
