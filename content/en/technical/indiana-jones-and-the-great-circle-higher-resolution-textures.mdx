<Section icon={Shield} title="Platform-Specific Texture Management">
The availability and management of higher resolution textures can vary slightly depending on your gaming platform. While PC offers granular control through in-game settings, consoles often handle this differently.

On Xbox Series X, players have reported an optional "higher resolution textures pack" that needs to be manually ticked for installation. This suggests that for some console versions, these packs might not be automatically included, potentially to save install size or cater to different console models (like the Series S).

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Check for Optional Packs (Consoles)">
    For Xbox Series X users, navigate to the game's "Manage game and add-ons" section. Look for an optional **higher resolution textures pack** and ensure it is selected for installation. This might not be automatically installed via Smart Delivery.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust Texture Pool Size (PC)">
    On PC, access the in-game graphics settings. Locate the **Texture Pool Size** option. If your GPU has 8GB of VRAM, start with 'Low' or 'Medium' and observe performance. For GPUs with more VRAM, you can experiment with 'High' or 'Supreme'.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Utilize Console Commands (PC)">
    To address LOD pop-in, which can detract from the visual quality even with high-res textures, open the in-game console by pressing the tilde (~) key. Type `r_lodscale` followed by a value like `3` or `5`. This can significantly reduce pop-in without a major performance hit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Monitor Performance">
    After adjusting any texture settings, always monitor your game's performance. Use an FPS counter and keep an eye on VRAM usage. If you experience stutters or significant frame drops, consider lowering the Texture Pool Size or other VRAM-intensive settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Map} title="Impact of Ray Tracing and Upscaling on Texture Presentation">
Ray Tracing and upscaling technologies, while not directly related to texture resolution, significantly influence how these textures are rendered and perceived in **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle**.

The game's extensive use of Ray-Traced Global Illumination (RTGI) enhances lighting and shadows, making textures appear more grounded and realistic. However, enabling full Path Tracing features, especially RT Sun Shadows, comes with a substantial performance and VRAM cost. This can indirectly force players to compromise on texture quality if their system can't handle both.

Upscaling solutions like DLSS (the only one currently available in-game) can help achieve playable frame rates at higher resolutions, but they are not without their drawbacks. DLSS can sometimes introduce visual artifacts like flickering black dots or shimmering, which can slightly detract from the crispness of high-resolution textures.

| Feature                       | Primary Effect                     | VRAM Impact | Performance Cost (Approx.) | Texture Perception             |
| :---------------------------- | :--------------------------------- | :---------- | :------------------------- | :----------------------------- |
| **Ray Traced Sun Shadows**    | Eliminates flickering/low-quality shadows | Very High   | ~50% drop                | Textures appear more grounded  |
| **Full Path Tracing**         | Comprehensive lighting and reflection | Extreme     | Massive drop               | Highly realistic texture interaction |
| **DLSS (Upscaling)**          | Renders at lower internal res, upscales | Moderate    | Significant gain           | Can introduce artifacts/shimmering |
| **Frame Generation**          | Generates interpolated frames      | High        | Improves smoothness, higher input lag | No direct texture impact, but higher VRAM can affect texture settings |
