In the world of archaeology, knowledge is just as dangerous as a loaded revolver. In Indiana Jones and the Great Circle, your progression is tied directly to the discovery of ancient texts and field manuals. These **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle adventure books** serve as the backbone of the game's RPG-lite system, allowing players to customize Indy’s combat prowess, stealth capabilities, and physical endurance. Unlike traditional leveling systems, you won't gain power simply by defeating enemies; you must explore every nook and cranny of the Vatican, Gizeh, and Sukhothai to find these hidden volumes. This guide provides a comprehensive breakdown of where to find the most impactful adventure books, how to manage your Adventure Points, and which skills will turn you into a legendary explorer in the 2026 gaming landscape.

<Section icon={BookOpen} title="Understanding Adventure Book Mechanics">

Before diving into specific locations, it is vital to understand how the upgrade loop works. Finding a book is only the first half of the process. Once a volume is added to your collection, it appears in the "Books" tab of your Journal. From there, you must "purchase" the upgrade using the currency you’ve earned through exploration.

| Upgrade Currency | Primary Source | Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Adventure Points (AP)** | Finding relics, taking photos, solving mysteries | Unlocks combat, stealth, and fitness skills |
| **Medicine Bottles** | Hidden in regional hubs (Vatican, Gizeh, etc.) | Specifically used for **Moxie** and **Shaping Up** |

<Callout type="tip" title="Maximizing AP Gain">
Adventure Points are rewarded for almost every "archaeological" action. To ensure you have enough points to unlock every book you find, keep your camera out. Photographing inscriptions and landmarks is the fastest way to farm AP early in the game.
</Callout>

The game features 55 unique ability books in total. While some are handed to you during the main story, the vast majority are hidden behind environmental puzzles, locked safes, or are sold by specific regional vendors like Ernesto in the Vatican or Tongdang in Sukhothai.

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Vatican City: Essential Early Game Books">

The Vatican acts as your first major open-area hub, and it is packed with foundational **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle adventure books**. These books focus on "Brawling" and "Fitness," which are essential since you won't have a large arsenal of firearms early on.

<Callout type="success" title="The Stealth Meta">
If you prefer a quiet approach, look for the **Throttle** book in the Borgia Courtyards. It costs 200 AP and allows Indy to knock out unaware lightweight and medium-weight enemies with a single whip pull, making guarded Vatican sections much easier to navigate.
</Callout>

Beyond combat, the Vatican also introduces "Exploration Books." These are unique because they don't give Indy a new move; instead, they update your map with markers for other collectibles.

| Exploration Book | How to Obtain | Map Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vatican Books** | Bought from Ernesto | Reveals all Adventure Book locations in the Vatican |
| **Vatican Mysteries** | Earned through puzzles | Marks all Mystery locations on the map |
| **Vatican Relics** | Found in Apostolic Palace | Highlights Ancient Relics for 100% completion |

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Gizeh and the Desert Upgrades">

As you transition to the Gizeh hub, the **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle adventure books** begin to focus on "Combat" and "Survival" against tougher Nazi forces. The verticality of the pyramids and the open desert require better traversal and crowd control skills.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Acquire the Lighter">
    Many books in Gizeh are hidden in dark tomb corners. You must progress the story until you obtain the lighter to explore these areas effectively.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Locate Jumbo Lasso">
    Head to the **Tomb of Khentkawes**. This book costs 325 AP and is a game-changer, allowing you to whip-pull heavyweight enemies into a grab.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Solve the Cloud Atlas">
    Visit the Meteorological Station. By matching dates to cloud types, you can unlock the **Gizeh Medicine** book, which is required to spend Medicine Bottles in this region.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Visit the Nazi Rec Zone">

  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Regional Currencies">

</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="The Medicine Bottle System: Moxie and Shaping Up">

Unlike standard skills, your actual Health and Stamina bars are upgraded through a specific set of **Indiana Jones and the Great Circle adventure books** found in medical huts or post offices. These upgrades are tiered (I, II, and III) and require **Medicine Bottles** instead of AP.

| Book Name | Tier | Requirement | Permanent Bonus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Moxie** | I | 5 Bottles | Initial stamina bar extension |
| **Moxie** | II | 10 Bottles | Secondary stamina bar extension |
| **Moxie** | III | 15 Bottles | Maximum stamina capacity |
| **Shaping Up** | I | 5 Bottles | First extra health reserve bar |
| **Shaping Up** | II | 10 Bottles | Second extra health reserve bar |
| **Shaping Up** | III | 15 Bottles | Maximum health reserves |

<Callout type="info" title="Where to Spend Bottles">
You can turn in your bottles at the **Vatican Post Office**, the **Gizeh Workers Area**, or the **Sukhothai Medical Hut**. Each location has a doctor or attendant who facilitates these specific upgrades.
</Callout>

</Section>
