<Section icon={Swords} title="Gameplay Mechanics: Whips, Stealth, and Puzzles">

Gameplay in Indiana Jones and the Great Circle is a hybrid experience. While primarily a first-person action-adventure game, it seamlessly transitions to a third-person perspective during specific traversal moments, such as swinging across chasms or climbing ledges. This allows players to appreciate the iconic silhouette of Indy while maintaining the immersion of a first-person perspective for combat and puzzle-solving.

Combat is designed around the idea that Indy is a survivor, not a superhero. While firearms are available, the game heavily encourages stealth and the use of the environment. You can pick up everyday objects like shovels, frying pans, and broomsticks to fend off enemies.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Technical Performance on Nintendo Switch 2">

In 2026, the Nintendo Switch 2 version of the game is a major focus for many Indiana Jones and the Great Circle reviews. Bringing a visually intensive Bethesda title to a portable platform required significant optimization. The game utilizes DLSS (Deep Learning Super Sampling) to maintain a playable experience while preserving visual fidelity.

While the game is undeniably beautiful, with character likenesses that look exactly like a young Harrison Ford and the late Tony Todd, there are some technical trade-offs to be aware of.
