<Section icon={Swords} title="Perspective and Improvised Combat">

One of the most discussed aspects of the game is its perspective. While many expected a third-person adventure similar to *Uncharted*, MachineGames chose a first-person view to maximize immersion. This allows players to see the world through Indy's eyes, focusing on the tactile nature of his tools. However, the game seamlessly transitions to third-person during specific actions to maintain the cinematic feel of the films.

| Action Type | Perspective | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Combat & Shooting | First-Person | Precision and immersion |
| Puzzle Interaction | First-Person | Detail-oriented observation |
| Whip Traversal | Third-Person | Spatial awareness and cinematic flair |
| Climbing & Ledging | Third-Person | Visualizing Indy's movement |
| Swimming | Third-Person | Navigational clarity |

Combat in *The Great Circle* is less about being a super-soldier and more about being a survivor. While Indy has his revolver, ammo is intentionally scarce, forcing players to use the environment. You can pick up almost any object—bottles, rolling pins, frying pans, or even fly swatters—to use as a melee weapon. These items are often "improvisational," meaning they break after use, encouraging a constant rotation of tactics.

<Section icon={Camera} title="Exploration, Puzzles, and The Journal">

Exploration is the heart of the experience. The game is divided into various regions, some of which are linear story paths, while others are wide-open hubs that encourage backtracking and discovery. To aid in this, Indy uses a camera—a tool that wasn't just invented for the game but serves as a primary way to document the journey.

Taking photos of objectives, ancient inscriptions, or suspicious landmarks adds them to Indy's Journal. Unlike other games that use generic stock photos, *The Great Circle* uses the actual photo the player took, complete with the specific angle and lighting. This journal acts as your primary guide for solving the game's many puzzles.

**Essential Exploration Tools:**
- **The Camera**: Used to identify points of interest and solve visual puzzles.
- **The Lighter**: Essential for dark tombs and scaring away small threats.
- **The Journal**: Stores maps, photos, and clues collected during the campaign.
